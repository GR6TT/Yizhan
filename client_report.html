Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?
I selected content that matched the core theme of the site and my personal positioning, and the client also provided the images I needed to use.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?
I communicated with the developer during class and via emails. There were minor misunderstandings on individual details in the process, which were resolved through timely supplementary communication.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?
Generally, the developer met my basic expectations for the site’s functions and style, and also added some small optimizations that improved the user experience.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?
Next time, I will sort out more detailed requirement standards in advance to reduce the need for later revisions and make the cooperation more efficient.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior devs?
I communicated with the team during class, sent emails for formal notifications, and collaborated with junior developers through GitHub to assign and track tasks.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?
I kept in touch with the client through in-class communication and emails. Occasionally, there were delays in requirement confirmation and minor misunderstandings on individual details.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?
I published the client’s site via GitHub and shared the access link and related information with the client through emails.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?
Using GitHub for site management was generally smooth, and the version control function helped avoid confusion in code updates, with only a short learning curve for permission settings.

Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?
I followed the team’s communication rules to report progress to the lead. There were minor ambiguities in individual task requirements, which were clarified through one-on-one communication.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?
The team lead provided clear task guidelines, so I had a clear understanding of the functional and style requirements for each page I was responsible for.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?
I completed the core content of the assigned pages as required, and also put forward some small optimization suggestions that were adopted by the team.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?
I encountered small problems with code merging at first, but with the lead’s guidance, I quickly mastered the process and did not have similar issues later.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?
The biggest challenge was multi-task collaboration as a client, a team member and a team leader. I successfully overcame it and gained a lot from the experience.

What did you enjoy most about this project?
I enjoyed the process of team collaboration the most, and the sense of accomplishment from seeing the final site go live was very rewarding.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?
If I could redo it, I would start the preparation work earlier and reserve more buffer time for unexpected situations to make the process more relaxed.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.
Yes, this experience improved my practical operation and communication abilities, and made me more confident in dealing with similar projects in the future.
